My Story

Fun fact (and not on my resume): I started a master’s degree in city planning, but I quit. I wanted to improve the human experiences in public spaces, but the courses were unsatisfyingly theoretical. In the last twenty years, that same journey to impact the human experience has taken me from the arts (an actor), to the sciences (a civil engineer), to the classroom (a teacher), to the research space (an education and social science researcher). In UX, I believe I’ve found my tribe.

In addition to a well attested facility with crucial UX strategies and artifacts, including user flows, information architecture, competitive analyses and more, I bring equally valuable assets honed over this unconventional professional journey. I am a restless creative, an analytical thinker, a goal-driven designer, a confident communicator, and a lifelong learner. I am also bilingual in Spanish with experience living and working abroad.

As an emerging UX designer and researcher, I especially look forward to the earlier stages of the design lifecycle: discovery sessions with stakeholders, brainstorming workshops with the project team, competitive analysis, wireframing and experience design. As a lifelong learner, I also look forward to finding collaboration opportunities across teams and growing professionally where I’m planted.

On a personal note, I am an avid runner and a bit crazy about it. If you want to chat work and/or go for a run, shoot me an email!
